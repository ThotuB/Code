2.4 Weight decay

weight decay (generally called `2 regularization), might be the most widely-used technique

for regularizing parametric machine learning models

the technique is motivated by the basic intuition that the simplest models are the ones for

which some norm of their weight vector is as small as possible

the most common method for ensuring a small weight vector is to add its norm as a penalty

term to the problem of minimizing the loss

thus, we replace our original objective, minimizing the prediction loss on the training labels,

with the new objective, minimizing the sum of the prediction loss and the penalty term

now, if our weight vector grows too large, our learning algorithm might focus on minimizing

the weight norm vs. minimizing the training error, which is exactly what we want

when we introduced neural networks, we focused on linear models with a single output
